Can AI Replace A Human?

Nowadays, AI is becoming indispensable in everyday life and is already influencing almost every field — even music. AI can now not only recommend songs for you on Spotify, but it's also becoming a powerful tool that helps with vocal tuning and makes producing tracks cheaper and faster. Today, AI is capable of creating entire compositions, and all it needs is a simple prompt with your preferences (like Endel, Suno, or AIVA). You could say that thanks to this, even a beginner with no experience can now easily create music — maybe not perfect, but still music. Some predictions even say that the overall music market might grow by as much as 17%, which is a pretty impressive number, and shows that AI shouldn't be underestimated. Still, there's the question: can this kind of music truly compare to the music written by geniuses or people who dedicated their whole lives to it — like Mozart or Salieri in the book Amadeus? I don't think so. As I mentioned earlier, AI is just a tool meant to help and speed up the music-making process by handling most of the repetitive tasks. But even so, I think if things keep going the way they are, AI might eventually replace people and kill creativity altogether.
